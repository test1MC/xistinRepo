Container Gap

Container Gap refers to the gap that develops on the infeed of the machine between containers when there is a loss of back pressure.

Carrier

Carrier is the plastic material applied to containers to make packages.

Carrier Feed Drum

Carrier Feed Drum is the component on the machine that pulls the carrier from the reel stand and creates a tension-free loop of carrier prior to the feed tray.

Carrier Safety

Carrier Safety refers to the condition at the reel stand in which the demand for carrier exceeds the reel stand speed. In this condition, the dancer arm raises to its upper limit.

Cutoff

Cutoff is the section of the machine that cuts the continuous string of carrier applied containers into discrete packages.

Dancer Arm

Dancer Arm is the component on the reel stand that regulates the speed at which the reel hubs turn.

Fan Fold (option)

Fan Fold is the system component that holds a pallet of six cells of carrier and dispenses it to the machine.

Feed Tray

Feed Tray is the component on the machine that forms the carrier and applies it to the jaws.

Heat Seal

Heat Seal refers to the process of splicing the end of one carrier reel to the beginning of another.

Jaw

Jaw is the component on the jawdrum assembly that holds the carrier while it is being stretched.

Jawdrum

Jawdrum is the section of the machine that stretches the carrier and applies it to the containers.

Jawdrum/Infeed Starwheels

Jawdrum/Infeed Starwheels are the components on the machine that separate and control the containers while the carrier is applied.

Jog

Jog mode is when the machine operates at a slow speed as long as the Jog button is pushed.

Low Carrier

Low Carrier refers to the condition at the reel stand in which one of the reels is nearly empty.

No Containers

No Containers refers to the condition in which the supply of containers to the machine has been depleted.

No Carrier

No Carrier refers to the condition at the reel stand in which the carrier on one of the reels runs out before the splice is made.

Downstream Stop

Downstream Stop refers to the condition in which the downstream equipment is not running and the packages have accumulated on the downstream infeed conveyor.

Prime

Prime refers to the machine operation mode in which the machine runs the machine conveyor and turner/diverter to prime the infeed if containers are supplied by the customer infeed conveyors. This is a time function after the Prime button is pressed. The customer outfeed must be able to accept packages from the machine.

Reel Stand (Option)

Reel Stand is the system component that holds two reels of carrier and dispenses it to the machine.

Run

Run mode is when the machine starts, stops, and changes speeds as line conditions indicate.

Timing

Timing refers to the relationship between the containers and machine components.

Turner/Diverter

Turner/Diverter is the section of the machine that orients and/or positions the packages for transfer onto the downstream equipment’s infeed conveyor.

Machine Introduction

Intended Use of the Hi-Cone Model 8M42

The Hi-Cone 8M42 applies a polyethylene carrier to containers and separates them into packs. The machine automatically responds to line conditions, starting and stopping as necessary. Packaging rate is determined automatically from the availability of containers within prescribed limits.

Responsible Packaging

ITW Hi-Cone recognizes that we all share a responsibility to sustain our environment. Manufacturers and consumers must take a hard look at the choice of packaging on products we buy and sell.

Since the introduction of the Hi-Cone carrier in the early 1960s, they have represented minimal packaging and less waste. Studies show that nothing breaks down quickly in our landfills, including paper. Of the three major packaging types used for beverage multipacks (plastic shrink film, paperboard, ring carriers), ring carriers offer the greatest opportunity to reduce volume into landfills. Minimizing the amount of packaging sent to waste landfills is important. The less material going into landfills, the better.

While ring carriers make up only a small portion of the litter problem, they too should be recycled or disposed of properly. To minimize careless litter, ITW Hi-Cone developed a light sensitive (photodegradable) ring carrier which breaks down naturally when exposed to sunlight. Hi-Cone carriers are non-toxic, breaking down into smaller pieces of inert, harmless material. Since 1988, every ring carrier we sold in North America (and globally since 1992) has been photodegradable.

Ring carriers, compared with plastic shrink film and paperboard, almost always generate the least amount of solid and water-born waste. The ring carriers also use less energy and release the fewest emissions into the environment, continuing a long standing commitment to sustainability and environmental stewardship.

Hi-Cone collects used ring carriers throughout North America through its Ring Leader Recycling Program. Recycled rings are processed and converted into material for new rings and other products. Made of photosensitive low density polyethylene, our carriers can be combined with other compatible plastics and recycled into a variety of different products.

To learn more about Hi-Cone, a worldwide leader in sustainable, plastic packaging, visit www.hi-cone.com. If you would like to learn more about ring carriers and the environment or if recycling for ring carriers (made of #4 plastics) does not exist in your area and you would like to start a recycling program for them, visit www.ringleader.com.
