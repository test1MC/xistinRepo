Reel Stand and Fanfold

The Reel Stand supplies carrier to the machine with minimal tension in the web. As the dancer arm on the reel stand raises, the speed of the reel is increased to keep up with demand. Placing the reel stand close to the machine is important because it minimizes the need for additional rollers and/or troughs to support the carrier. Each additional carrier support increases the tension in the carrier at the machine which may affect performance.

Refer to pages 3-14 through 3-16 for details.

Requirements may vary.

Transport the optional Reel Stand and/or Fanfold units and locate in place designated by the line layout.

Anchor the unit to the floor after final startup.

The customer is to supply a conduit from the heat sealer on the Fanfold unit to the Fanfold connection point on the Reel Stand or machine.

Reel Stand

The reel stand can be located at the infeed or the outfeed on either side of the machine, but must be specified at the time the machine is ordered.

The angle may vary from parallel to 15 degrees. A slight angle is recommended to ease the entry path of the carrier to the machine.

Generally, the reel stand is located on the operator side of the
Hi-Cone machine.

Decals showing the carrier feed path are applied on the reel stand and application machine.

Anchor the reel stand to the floor after final startup.

Fanfold

All dimensions are for reference only. Final dimensions may vary.

Make sure the fanfold stand is bolted to the floor before operation. The customer is to supply hardware required for floor mounting.

Fanfold gaylords average 800 pounds and require a fork truck to place in the fanfold unit.

The customer is to supply conduit from the heat sealer on the fanfold unit to the fanfold limit switch connection on the reel stand or machine.

Discharge Safety Sensor Installation

The Discharge Safety Sensor must be mounted on the customer supplied discharge conveyor a minimum of 2 ft. from the end of the machine frame, as shown below.

The sensor is provided with a universal mount that is adaptable to a variety of conveyor designs.

Drill 8 holes in customer conveyor to mount sensor assembly.

If desired, trim excess length from mounting rods.

The photoeye cable must be terminated in the junction box located on the discharge end of the machine.

The discharge safety sensor may be located inside the discharge tunnel, if it fits. If it is not desirable to be inside the tunnel, it may be mounted further downstream.

Safety Processor Password Policy

It is Hi-Cone policy to restrict the sharing of processor passwords on any machine using a safety processor. Any program changes that require off-line modification to the program, and subsequent downloading of the program into the processor, requires that a Hi-Cone service team member be present at the time of the change. This policy is to protect machine operators and maintenance teams from harm that may be caused by modifications to the safety routine or conditions resulting from an unlocked program.

Control Wiring

For UL/CSA machines, the Downstream Backup sensor is provided with a 15 m, four-wire cable. Additional wire to extend the cable should be 22 awg.

The reel stand communicates with the main enclosure via Ethernet/IP, using a CAT5 Ethernet cable.

For CE machines, the reel stand communicates with the main enclosure via Profinet.

A 5’ (1.5 m) section of flexible conduit should be provided to allow the position of the reel stand to be adjusted slightly during machine start-up.

Move and Store

 

 

 

Immediate danger to life.

The machine may tilt if not observing the center of gravity or the forklift points. Failure to observe may result in immediate danger to life and/or major damage to equipment.

Before moving the machine from its production site, do the following:

Check that the machine can be transported without any problems with the ceiling height, door, and passages.

Make sure the machine can be lifted in accordance with Section 3.1.3.1 Moving Skidded Equipment.

Disassemble Machine

 

 

 

Risk of personal injury.

Before disassembling the machine, read Chapter 2 Safety. If safety precautions are not followed, there is risk of personal injury.

Risk of damage to the equipment.

Make sure that Section 3.3.2 Utilities is followed.

In order to simplify re-assembly of the machine, put all loosened screws, nuts, washers, and so on in plastic bags and mark them.

Clean the machine.

Set the main power disconnect switch in the OFF position and lock it with a padlock.

Turn off the electrical power supply upstream from the machine.

Disconnect the power and utility lines. Disconnect at power terminals and remove cable from the electrical cabinet.

Plug or protect all open connections.

Loosen the screw terminals on the disconnect and remove the cable from the main electrical enclosure.

Disconnect and remove either the line controller communications cables or the relay communications cables.

Remove the photoeyes and sensors from upstream and downstream conveyor. Bundle the wires and tie-wrap them to the Hi-Cone machine.

Loosen the bolts securing the indicating tower.

Disconnect any external infeed or outfeed conveyors connected to the Hi-Cone machine.

Disconnect the Hi-Cone infeed conveyor from the main machine. Transport the infeed conveyor and machine separately.
