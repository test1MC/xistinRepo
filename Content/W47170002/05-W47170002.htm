General

This chapter is organized by functional area of the machine.

If a procedure in this chapter is indicated to be part of changeover, refer to the Changeover Screens on the HMI for the recommended sequence of changeover.

The changeover sequence can differ depending upon the container diameter, container height, and carrier type.

Infeed Conveyor

Inverted Container Sensors - Adjust

The Inverted Container sensors detect containers on the infeed conveyor.

The machine stops when the sensors detect one of the following conditions: a gap between containers, an upside down container, a missing container.

Follow these steps to adjust the sensors:

Loosen the handle (a).

Position the head of the proximity sensors (b) 0.125 to 0.25 in (3.18 to 6.35 mm) above the container lid and tighten the handle (a).

Sensors too far from the container lid cause nuisance stops while sensors too close to the container lid cause the containers to jam.

Adjust the photoeye sensors (c) so they detect the reflectors (d) mounted to the center lane divider (e).

 

Container Guides Rails

The container guide rails control the containers on the infeed conveyor.

Container Guide Rails - Adjust Height

Position the top guide rail 3.13 in (79.5 mm) above the tabletop chain for most container heights.

For very short containers, lower the rail to maintain contact with the container shoulder.

Follow these steps to adjust the height of the guide rails:

Loosen the nut (a) on the mounting bracket (b).

Adjust the guide rail (c) as required.

Tighten the nut (a).

 

Container Guide Rails - Adjust Width

Follow these steps to adjust the width of the container guide rails:

Loosen the set screws (a) in the inner (b) and outer (c) collars.

Place the smallest container on the infeed conveyor (d).

Loosen the adjusting handle (e).

Push the container guides (f) in until they are approximately 0.0625 in (1.6 mm) away from the container.

Tighten the adjusting handle (e).

Move the outer collar (c) until it is flush with the adjustment bracket (g).

Tighten the set screw (a) in the outer collar (c).

Repeat this sequence using the larger container to set the inner collar (b).

Once the collars are set, loosen the adjustment handle and move the guides in or out to the appropriate stop collar to make adjustments between container sizes.

Container Cleanout Device

The container cleanout device is used to provide the necessary back pressure behind containers left on the infeed conveyor during the container cleanout process. Without back pressure provided by the device, the containers jam and break open as they enter the starwheels.

Container Cleanout Device - Set

Follow these steps to set the container cleanout device:

Press Runout to jog the containers to the brackets (a) indicated below.

Place the container cleanout device (b) on the conveyor (c) behind the containers.

Press Runout to run out the containers.

The container cleanout device (b) stops when it comes in contact with the stopping post (d).

 

Container Cleanout Device - Change Rear Weight

Changeover Type: Container Diameter

Follow these steps to change the rear weight:

Turn the knob (a) until the rear weight (b) is free.

Remove the rear weight (b) from the cleanout device (c).

Reverse these steps to install the rear weight (b).

 

Infeed Timing Screws

Infeed Timing Screws - Remove

Changeover Type: Container Diameter

Loosen the locking handle (a).

Slide the drive unit (b) away from the screw (c).

Pull the screw (c) until it is disengaged from the pin on the post (d). Remove the screw (e) and store.

Repeat the steps above to remove the timing screw on the other side.

 

Infeed Timing Screws - Install

Changeover Type: Container Diameter

The discharge end of each timing screw (a) is engraved with the hand and product.

Align the screw (a) with the pin of the post (b) and push the screw (a) into position.

Place the screw (a) onto the cradle (c) of the drive unit (d).

Manually rotate the screw (a) so the drive unit pin is aligned with the receiver slots in the screw.

Push the drive unit (d) to engage the screw (a).

Tighten the locking handle (f).

Repeat the steps above to install the timing screw on the other side.

 

Infeed Timing Screws - Check Container Gap Sensor

The infeed screw container gap sensor detects gaps, triggers faults, and stops the machine.

Check that the photoeye bracket (a) is dim A from the other bracket (b). If not, adjust accordingly.

Jawdrum - Adjust Timing

The jawdrum performs the following actions when changeover is complete:

The turner/diverter and starwheels rotate until they locate their home.

The jawdrum turns until its home proximity sensor (a) detects the homing actuator block (b).

The jawdrum then performs another move to an offset value designated for the carrier the machine is set to run. This offset value is shown in the Jawdrum Adjust screen.

 

 

 

If timing adjustments are needed to improve carrier application, they can be made from the Jawdrum Adjust Screen.

On the HMI, navigate to the Jawdrum Adjust Screen.

Press the clockwise and counterclockwise buttons to advance or retard the jawdrum and achieve the desired jawdrum timing.

The Drum Advance button (a) moves the jaws in the direction of the machine flow. The Drum Retard button (b) moves the jaws in the direction opposite the machine flow.

Adjustment to the jawdrum rotational timing must be done with the carrier on the jaws and the containers in the jawdrum starwheels.

 

Jawdrum - Adjust Height

The height of the jawdrum must be adjusted to compensate for changes in the elevation of the desired carrier height on the container.

The jawdrum height values are calculated automatically when changeover is complete. The servo motor (a) and lift jacks (b) move to repeatable positions.

 

 

 

If height adjustments are needed to improve carrier application, they can be made from the Jawdrum Adjust screen.

On the HMI, navigate to the Jawdrum Adjust screen.

Press the Up or Down arrows to achieve the desired jawdrum height.               

The new height settings are automatically saved for the current package configuration when exiting the screen.

Rim Applied Carrier (RAC) - Overview

The relationship between the carrier aperture holes formed on the jawdrum and the containers passing under the jawdrum is critical to obtain maximum package integrity.

At the point where the carrier band intersects the container lid, the front of the container lid should be dimension A behind the trailing edge of the stretched carrier. If the distances are not the same for both lanes of containers, the jawdrum starwheel timing must be adjusted. See 5.4.2 Jawdrum - Adjust Timing.

As the containers and carrier continue to move through the machine, a portion of the carrier lays over the back of the container lid. This carrier “snaps” under the container lid near the bottom of the jawdrum.

 

 

Stripper Shoe (RAC)

Stripper Shoe (RAC) - Set

The stripper shoe aids the completion of the carrier application by pulling the carrier off the jaws.

Adjust the jawdrum height as required to achieve the correct depth of application. See 5.4.3 Jawdrum - Adjust Height.

Adjust the stripper shoe dimension [A] from the top of the can. To adjust the clearance, shim as required between the stripper shoe mount and the stripper shoe.

Center the stripper shoe assembly between the jaw opening with dimension [B] clearance between the outside of the stripper plate and the inside of the jaw.

 

Stripper Shoe (RAC) - Remove/Install

The stripper shoe assembly is heavy. Use caution during removal and installation.

Removing Stripper Shoe

Loosen the four adjustable handles (a).

Slide the stripper shoe assembly (b) toward the discharge until it clears the mounting clamp (c).

 

 

Installing Stripper Shoe

Loosen the four adjustable handles (a).

Lift the stripper shoe assembly (b) and position it in front of the mounting clamp (c).

Slide the stripper shoe assembly (b) toward the infeed until it stops.

Slide the gauge (e) between the stripper shoe (g) and the jaws (f).

The assembly centers itself on the stopping pins (d).

Pull the assembly toward the jaws (f) to make sure the assembly is square and flush against the jaws (f).

Tighten the adjustable handles (a) and remove the gauge (e).

 

Stripper Shoe (RAC) - Adjust Auxiliary Container Guides

The auxiliary container guides provide additional control of the container during application. The container guides should position the containers so that the lids do not interfere with the carrier strippers.

Side Applied Carrier (SAC) - Overview

The relationship between the carrier aperture holes formed on the jawdrum and the containers passing under the jawdrum is critical to obtain maximum package integrity.

During successful carrier application, the carrier bands are positioned between the moving containers. At the point where the carrier band intersects the container lid, the carrier band lays over the back of the container lid. The amount of carrier band interference with the container lid varies with application depth (larger application depth have more interference). The amount of band interference may appear excessive at the intersection point, but as the containers and carrier continue to move through the machine, the container is accelerated by the starwheels so that the carrier band slides over the back of the lid and is pushed down the container body.

Feed Drum Carrier Feed - Overview

The feed drum carrier feed system isolates tension variations in the carrier between the reelstand or fanfold and the jawdrum.

Using pinch rollers and a dancer arm, carrier is fed to the jawdrum with an appropriate amount of tension. The pinch roller design also allows the same assembly to run carriers of varying pitch.

The speed of the carrier feed system follows the speed of the jawdrum plus or minus a trimming value determined by the dancer arm location.

The dancer arm raises and lowers as tension in the carrier increases and decreases, respectively. An analog sensor and cam attached to the dancer arm are used to adjust the carrier feed system speed as the carrier tension varies. As a safety, there is a High Limit proximity sensor and Low Limit proximity sensor that stops the machine should the carrier tension become too much or too little.

After reel B (g) is empty, turn the A-B selector switch (j) on the reel stand operator station to the A position. This changes the internal drive to power the A hub rather than the B hub.

Reel Stand - Adjust Low Carrier Sensor

The carrier near the end of the reel is back wound so the tail drops out before the carrier supply is completely diminished. The Low Carrier photoeye is activated when the tail breaks the photoeye beam. Adjust the photoeye so it sees the reflector.

Follow these steps to adjust the Low Carrier sensor:

Loosen the hardware on the photoeye mounting bracket.

Adjust the position of the photoeye (a) until it is aligned with the reflector (b).

Tighten the mounting hardware.

 

Reel Stand - Check No Carrier Sensor

The No Carrier at Reel Stand sensor (a) is located above the rollers of the reel stand. If the end of the carrier passes the sensor (a), the machine stops.

Check the function and position of the sensor.

The tail of the whisker switch needs to be adjusted so the carrier path is directly under the switch. This position ensures that the loose tail of carrier engages the switch.

 

Reel Stand - Check Speed Sensor

The speed at which the reel hubs (a) turn is determined by the position of the dancer roller (b). As the dancer roller (b) moves up and down, the distance from the sonic sensor (c) varies. The output of the sensor (c) controls the speed of the motor in the reel stand.

Check the function and position of the sensor (c).

Reel Stand - Adjust Carrier Safety Sensor

The Carrier Safety sensor turns the Hi-Cone off if the reel stand is not able to keep up with the carrier demands of the machine.

Set the sensor to activate when the dancer roller raises to the upper limit.

With the dancer arm raised to the upper mechanical limit, follow these steps to adjust the Carrier Safety sensor:

Loosen the nut (a) on the proximity sensor (b) and adjust until the red light on the side of the sensor (b) illuminates.

Tighten the nut (a) on the proximity sensor (b).

 

Reel Stand - Adjust Drive Enable Sensor

The Drive Enable sensor cuts power to the reel stand drive motor when the dancer roller is at the lower mechanical limit. If the sensor does not activate, the reel hub may continue to turn when the dancer arm is at the lower limit.

With the dancer arm lowered to the lower mechanical limit, follow these steps to adjust the Drive Enable sensor:

Loosen the nut (a) on the sensor (b) and adjust until the red light on the side of the sensor (b) illuminates.

Tighten the nut (a) on the sensor (b).

The Hi-Cone product label provides all the necessary information to isolate any defective carriers. Each of the following items should be used to determine what product should be isolated as defective.

Press Number

Shift of Manufacture

Press Lane

Date of Manufacture

Hi-Cone product is produced in parallel lanes. Often carrier quality issues are isolated to a single lane. When a quality problem is identified, it is important to check each production lane for the defect.

Once a defective lane is identified, that lane should be bypassed by separating the carriers from the defective lane from the other lanes. The operator can then splice together the good lanes.
