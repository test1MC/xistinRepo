General

 

 

 

Incorrect use of the equipment will put your life in danger.
 will take no responsibility for any accidents, injuries to personnel or damage to the machine if the equipment is installed, operated, or serviced without following the instructions in this Manual.

 

 

 

Incorrect use of the Safety System will put your life in danger.
Never jog or run the machine if any safety component is non-operational. Change all non-operational safety components immediately.

Safety Message Descriptions

Safety messages in this document have the following significance:

 

 

 

Risk of Immediate danger to life.
Danger indicates an imminently hazardous situation which, if not avoided, will result in death or serious injury.


 

 

Risk of serious personal injury.
Warning indicates a potentially hazardous situation which, if not avoided, could result in death or serious injury.

Risk of minor personal injury or damage to equipment.
Caution indicates a potentially hazardous situation which, if not avoided, may result in minor or moderate injury. It may also be used to alert against unsafe practices. It could also indicate a potentially hazardous situation which may result, if not avoided, in property damage.

Important is used to emphasize information for proper use of the machine.

A note emphasizes useful information. A note is not to be regarded as hazard information.

Personnel Requirements

It is the responsibility of the lessee of the machine to ensure that machine operators and maintenance personnel have received instruction specific to the safe operation of the machine.

During maintenance or service work, the service technician and the electrician are responsible not only for the machine, but also for the personnel in the vicinity of the machine.

It is also the responsibility of the service technician and the electrician to assure that the machine safety devices are fully operational before they finish the maintenance or service work.

Operator

Operation of the equipment is for trained persons only. The machine operator is an individual who has enough training and/or knowledge to run the equipment in the correct way.

Only allow trained persons and service technicians to work on the equipment.

Service Technicians

Service technicians may be:

Technicians employed by Hi-Cone or

Employees who have gone through training or have an equivalent knowledge.

Technicians employed by customer.

Technicians employed by the customer must have the following skills:

ability to read technical information

ability to understand technical drawings

knowledge of basic mechanics and electricity

knowledge of mathematics

ability to handle special tools

Electricians

Electricians should:

be certified according to local regulations

have experience with similar types of equipment

have proven skills in reading and working from drawings and cable diagrams

The use of a key or tool is necessary for access to the electrical cabinet by skilled persons to carry out operations for which it may not be appropriate to disconnect power from the equipment. Operate the Main Power Disconnect as needed while the door is open.

Examples of such operations are:

resetting of protective devices

adjustment of device settings

fault finding and diagnostic testing

Emergency Stop

 

 

Hazardous voltage.

Will shock, burn, or cause death.

Hazardous voltage remains ON after activating an Emergency Stop.

Emergency stop devices are used to stop this equipment immediately in a hazardous situation. Learn the positions of all emergency stop devices and how to use them.

Instructions for a normal production stop are included in Section 4.7.2 Normal Stop

Push an Emergency Stop button to stop this equipment immediately.

Main Power Disconnect

 

 

 

Hazardous voltage and moving machinery.
The electrical supply disconnecting device must be turned off and secured with a lock before any maintenance. The key to the lock must be removed by the technician and retained in his/her possession until all work is completed.

 

 

 

Risk of electrical shock during discharge time.

After the main power disconnect has been turned OFF, high voltage can still remain in the electrical cabinet. Discharge time is approximately five minutes.

 

 

 

Follow plant policy on lockout / tagout procedures.

Failure to observe plant policy can put you are risk of death or serious injury.

 

 

 

Risk of electrical shock.

Although power is removed from the panel. it still resides at the top of the Main Power Disconnect.

 

 

 

Do not by-pass the safety system and carry out any settings while the machine is in Production mode.

 

 

The Main Power Disconnect must be off before performing certain types of service, including:

disconnection of electrical cables

replacement of motors, etc.

service work in electrical cabinet, connection boxes, etc.

service work in machine sections not visible from the control panel

Certain adjustments require power including setting of photoeyes, proximity switches, frequency converters, etc. See 4.7.2 Normal Stop to stop the machine normally before performing these tasks.

 

Doors and Guards

 

 

Moving parts and hot surfaces could cause serious injuries.
Never inch or run the machine if any safety switch is non-operational or any cover is not in position.

 

 

Do not bypass the safety system.

Never operate the machine with safety guards, shields, barriers, covers, alarms, or other protective devices disconnect, removed, or bypassed.

Equipment Damage.
The equipment can be damaged if it is not stopped in the correct way. Never stop this equipment by opening a movable guard, for example, a door or cover, equipped with an interlocking device.

 

Request to Enter

There is a Request to Enter button located on each door of the machine.

Use the Emergency Stop buttons only in case of emergencies. Use the Request to Enter buttons to access the machine under normal conditions.

Sequence

To open a door, push the white Request to Enter button (a).

A controlled stop of the machine is initiated.

The machine stops after a time delay, the door unlocks, and the indicator changes from flashing to solid.

Open the door to access the area and perform work.

Exit the area and close the door.

Push the blue Reset button (b) to lock the door.

Push the main Reset button to reset the machine’s master control relay and restore control power.

 

Electrical Cabinet

 

 

 

Risk of electrical shock.
There is high voltage inside the electrical cabinet. Risk of immediate danger to life through electrical shock. In case of accident, immediately call for medical attention.

 

 

 

Risk of electrical shock.
Lock the electrical cabinet door after performing any type of work in the electrical cabinet.

If the Main Power Disconnect is in the OFF position, high voltage can still remain in the electrical cabinet. Discharge time is approximately five minutes.

Only skilled or instructed persons can perform work inside the electrical cabinet.

 

Lockout Procedure

 

 

 

Incorrect use of the Safety System will put your life in danger.
Failure to follow the lockout procedure could result in death or serious injury.

This procedure establishes the minimum requirements for lockout of energy sources that could cause injury to personnel.

All employees are responsible for following the lockout/tagout procedure. Your company should instruct all employees in the safety significance of the lockout procedures. Your company or training liaison should instruct each new or transferred employee in the purpose and use of the lockout procedure.

To perform maintenance on the machine, use the lockout procedure to lockout the electrical energy source.

Notify all applicable employees that the machine is to undergo a lockout.

Turn off the main electrical power with the Main Power Disconnect located on the front of the electrical cabinet.

Padlock the switch.

illness.

Safety Precautions

 

 

 

Risk of serious personal injury.

This machine contains multiple moving parts and rotates at a high rate of speed. Failure to follow the safety recommendations in this manual could result in death or serious injury.

Safety devices mounted on the machine only form the basis of protection against accident, injury to personnel, and damage to the machine. Machine operators and maintenance personnel must be on guard for possible dangers:

For safety, observe guidelines for the proper handling and disposal of oils and lubricants.

Never wear neckties, scarves, gloves, or loose-fitting clothing.

Never wear jewelry - bracelets, watches, rings, or necklaces.

Restrain long hair.

Only operate the machine if it is in perfect running condition.

Be alert to the presence of others on or near the machine.

Check the area around the machine before any button is pushed.

Use eye protection.

Keep all operating elements - levers, grips, steps, platforms, etc. - free of dirt and grease to prevent slipping.

Keep the machine free of items that may obstruct or impede operation.

During operation, be attentive to excessive vibration, unusual sounds, or anything that may indicate problems requiring immediate action.

Inform Hi-Cone about any change occurring at the machine which might affect safety.

Never brake or slow down moving machinery with hands or with a makeshift device.

Do not engage in horseplay or behavior that may compromise safety.

 

 

Safety Summary

Be sure the following safety instructions are read, understood, and become a part of daily practice when operating or maintaining Hi-Cone equipment. The instructions act as a guide for prevention of personal injury and/or property damage but are no substitute for the operators attention to safety.

Never perform maintenance or repair work until the machine is disconnected and locked out.

Never attempt to operate the machine until you understand how it functions. If you are uncertain after studying the service manual, contact Hi-Cone service for assistance.

Never operate the machine unless all guards are in place. Do not attempt to make any safety device inoperative.

Never start the machine until you are certain that all personnel and foreign objects are clear.

The machine cycles automatically, be sure everyone is aware of the automatic cycling function.

Check all safety switches and other safety devices daily to ensure they operate correctly. Report any problems with safety systems immediately.

Never operate or perform maintenance or repair work on the machine when taking any kind of drug/sedative, under the influence of alcohol, or when over fatigued.

Never reach into moving parts to clear a jam of any type.

Keep the floor around the machine clean, dry, and free of obstructions. If water is necessary at the machine, wear shoes with slip resistant soles.

Always wear safety glasses.

Pay strict attention to all Caution, Warning, and Danger signs.

Adequate lighting must be available in the area where the machine is installed. Normal factory lighting is sufficient.

Always use manufactured or specified replacement parts.
